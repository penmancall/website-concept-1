Quickfold Chair.

A project for indn 342, 2017.

The aim of this project was to create a chair which embodies simplicity and pragmatic design philosophy.

Initial influence for the chair came from English designer Jasper Morrisonâ€™s plywood chair and his simplistic vision towards design.

The chair aims to speak about the most reduced and functional solutions to public seating.

The chair is formed from 1.5mm galvanised steel sheet, which provides a robust and durable surface that can be left outside and is resistant to the wear and tear which publicfurniture is subjected to.

Each of the right-angled folds in the material add stiffness and integrity to the structure, allowing the fine 1.5mm sheet to support bodyweight.

The manufacturing technique for the chair includes waterjet cutting and brake pressing. The process begins with a standard sized sheet of galvanised steel, measuring 2,438mm x 1,219mm.

A waterjet cutter must be used due to the folding tabs which would otherwise result in inaccurate cuts if machined with hand tools. Laser cutting is not appropriate for use with galvanised materials as the zinc layer can produce toxic fumes and flammable hydrogen gas as a by-product of the cutting process.
