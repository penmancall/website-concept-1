Quickfold Chair.

A project for indn 342, 2017.

The aim of this project was to create a chair which embodies simplicity and pragmatic design philosophy. Initial influence for the chair came from English designer Jasper Morrisonâ€™s plywood chair and his simplistic vision towards design. The chair aims to speak about the most reduced and functional solutions to public seating.

The chair is formed from 1.5mm galvanised steel sheet, which provides a robust and durable surface that can be left outside and is resistant to the wear and tear which publicfurniture is subjected to.

Each of the right-angled folds in the material add stiffness and integrity to the structure, allowing the fine 1.5mm sheet to support bodyweight. The manufacturing technique for the chair includes waterjet cutting and brake pressing. The process begins with a standard sized sheet of galvanised steel, measuring 2,438mm x 1,219mm.

A waterjet cutter must be used due to the folding tabs which would otherwise result in inaccurate cuts if machined with hand tools. Laser cutting is not appropriate for use with galvanised materials as the zinc layer can produce toxic fumes and flammable hydrogen gas as a by-product of the cutting process.

When the outlines have been cut, the prepared sheet is pressed into the final form with a hydraulic brake press or hand operated press. The front leg section of the chair folds inwards towards the centre, providing a stable base of support. The supporting members each have a 10mm right angled fold to provide further resistance to bowing and flexion under load.

To further prevent the legs from bowing, they meet the bottom of the chair and are situated within a narrow channel cut into the galvanised sheet. The backrest section of the chair provides support through the same stiffening techniques, with these folded members forming a junction with the backrest. Each fold extends inwards 70mm, providing ample support for flexion of the material.

Both the front legs and backrest sections are equipped with supporting tabs, which fold inward at right angles to further stiffen and increase support for members under compression when a load is applied to the chair. Final measurements of the chair after folding include 380mm leg height, 360mm depth and 360mm back support resulting in a total height of 740mm and width of 450mm
